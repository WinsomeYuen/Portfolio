Go Think Big

Who are Go Think Big? This was what the project revolved around. Trying to tell the story of who Go Think Big are, to those who don't know about them. What made the project unique was a group of us behind the creation were young people making content for young people. The final video was finally finished after numberous refinements from feedback from Go Think Big and O2. Collaboratively with the client, audience, and support from Given we created as a team what Go Think Big is.

Final Video

The final video was conceptualised by us the interns and we also directed it, whilst the editing was done by the agency through suggestions by us. Timeline

We were set one month and 2 days a week to complete the project. With such a short time frame and so much work to do, therefore a timeline was created to clearly outline the goal for each day. This timeline was created by the awesome team at Given who provided us with resources (cameramen, room space, recording equipment) that helped make the magic happen! Each day consisted of a presentation by a member a Given on the module for the day that will help us in the research and design process later in the day. At the end of everyday we would present our findings and get feedback to help refine ideas.

The Journey

Primary Research

We first started the project with research like most projects. We split up int goups to rsearch on 2 main topics:

Young People

Go Think Big

These were the 2 main focus points on the project and we further split this topic down to be more specific with our research. In order to best utilise the time we did quick 5 minute research on each topic and then swapped to a different one. The short amount of time meant that each person would research on a single point for each topic and narrow down the research before feedback at the end of the speed-research.

Interviews and Insights

Then we had to use our research to formulate questions for the depth-interviews. These were going to be with employees at O2 and GTB, as well as past participants and the general public who either was a young person or used O2 as their mobile network. After we collated the interview results we could develop insights to to structure and shortist the key concepts to incorporate in the video.
