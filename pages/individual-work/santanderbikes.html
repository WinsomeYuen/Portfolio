Santander Bikes (Interaction Design)

This coursework involved performing a short ethnographic study and applying some of the theories and frameworks discussed in the first few lectures for my interaction design module. In order to complete the coursework I needed to be familiar with the material from the associated lectures on Design Principles, Ethnography, Phenomenology and Activity Theory.

As a group, we made 2 visits to a Santander Cycles (London bike hire scheme) docking station outside waterloo station and made detailed, in-depth observations of peopleâ€™s interaction with each other and the system: the bikes, the docking point interfaces, the terminal screen, the maps and signage, their surroundings and any other devices or resources they use. I adopted an ethnographic approach: be open-minded and observe what actually happens, not just what I expected to happen; and try to make sense of why people behave as they do.

The main points to consider in our approach:
  • try to understand the interaction in the users own terms;
  • pay attention to the differences between individual and collaborative activity;
  • pay attention to the differences between private and public behaviours;
  • pay attention to how people manage the social context around them.
